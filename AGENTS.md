<2D Shooting Game – Player, Gameplay, and Design
The player is shown as a white rectangle at the bottom of the screen, standing on the ground. The player can move left and right using the arrow keys. When a key is held down, the player moves at a steady speed. The player cannot go past the edges of the screen.
The player can shoot by pressing the spacebar. When the player shoots, one bullet appears above them and moves straight upward. Only one bullet can be on the screen at a time. When the bullet reaches the top, it disappears.
Enemies fall from the top of the screen and move down toward the ground. They can look like simple red or dark shapes, such as small rectangles or basic alien icons. If an enemy reaches the ground (where the player is), the player loses health.
At the top of the screen, there is a scoreboard that shows how many enemies the player has hit. Next to the score, there are three hearts (♥♥♥) that show the player’s health. Each time an enemy reaches the ground, one heart is lost. When all three hearts are gone, the game is over.
The game runs in a loop, which keeps everything moving smoothly. In each loop:
The screen clears
The player moves based on key input
The bullet moves if it is active
Enemies move downward
Everything is drawn again (player, enemies, bullet, score, and hearts)
Visual Design Idea
Background: Blue sky with a flat ground at the bottom
Player: White rectangle or simple spaceship
Enemies: Red or dark shapes (like blocks or aliens)
Bullet: Small rectangle moving upward
Top of Screen: Score and three hearts>
